2. DEDUPLICATION IS THE PRIMARY INTEGRATION CHALLENGE
   100 results per query across 5 queries = up to 500 entries per poll,
   with massive cross-query overlap. The same event (e.g. Iran missile
   launch) will appear in "iran hormuz," "IRGC missile," and "iran strait
   oil" simultaneously, from 10-15 different outlets.
   Title-similarity clustering (not URL matching) is the right dedup
   approach — Google News redirect URLs are query-specific and will
   differ even for the same article.

3. GOOGLE NEWS LINK FORMAT — NOT DIRECT ARTICLE URLS
   Links are in the format: news.google.com/read/CBMi...
   These are Google redirect URLs, not direct article URLs.
   For dedup purposes, the source + title combination is the stable
   identifier. For scraping full content, the redirect must be followed
   to reach the actual article URL.

CONCLUSIONS:

1. NO PRACTICAL RATE LIMIT DETECTED
   Google News RSS is served from a heavily cached CDN. Repeated queries
   for the same search term return cached results almost instantly (~0.2s).
   43 requests in 76 seconds produced zero failures. Google appears to
   treat RSS feed fetches as lightweight read-only operations without
   meaningful per-IP throttling.

2. CACHING BEHAVIOR — IMPORTANT FOR FRESHNESS
   The fast ~0.2s response on repeat queries means Google is serving
   cached results, not live index queries. This has implications for
   how often new articles actually appear in repeated polls:
   - First fetch of a query: fresh from index (~0.5-0.7s)
   - Subsequent fetches: cached (~0.2s) — may not reflect articles
     published in the last few minutes
   Practical implication: polling faster than ~5-10 minutes is likely
   wasteful — the cache doesn't refresh that quickly. A 10-15 minute
   polling interval per query is probably optimal.

3. RECOMMENDED POLLING STRATEGY
   - 5 queries, staggered 2-3 minutes apart within each cycle
   - Full cycle every 10-15 minutes
   - No rate limit delay needed between requests
   - Monitor for empty responses or status changes over time —
     Google has not officially documented this RSS endpoint and
     could deprecate or throttle it without notice

4. RISK NOTE
   Google News RSS is undocumented and unofficial. It has worked reliably
   for years but has no SLA or guarantee. If it goes down, the fallback
   is the direct RSS feeds already in the collector (11 live feeds).
   Build the integration with this in mind — Google News is an enhancement,
   not a dependency.
